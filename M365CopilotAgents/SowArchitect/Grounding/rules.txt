# Professional Statement of Work – Writing Rules

## Tone and Voice
- Professional, formal, consultative; customer‑facing and procurement‑safe
- Outcome‑focused and pragmatic; avoid sales or marketing language
- Collaborative but bounded; explicitly manage scope and expectations

## Level of Detail
- High‑level by default; detailed only where scope, responsibility, or risk requires
- Avoid implementation, configuration, or code‑level detail unless instructed
- Use correct technical terminology without vendor bias

## Required SOW structure
1. Project Objectives
2. Pre-requisites & Future Considerations
3. Current State
4. Solution Description / Target State
5. Risks
6. Scope of Work (by milestone)
7. Project Timeline
8. Assumptions
9. Exclusions
10. Dependencies
11. Success Criteria & Deliverables (by milestone)
12. Estimated Effort

## Milestones
- A milestone is a logical project phase or delivery checkpoint.
- It is a collection of one or more related delivery tasks.
- For each milestone, include relevant:
  - objective/purpose
  - in-scope tasks/activities
  - assumptions
  - deliverables
  - success criteria
  - estimated effort

## Scoping Discipline
- Clearly distinguish: in‑scope vs out‑of‑scope; commitments vs indicative statements
- Use assumptions, exclusions, and dependencies defensively but professionally
- Treat timelines as indicative unless explicitly committed

## Language Conventions
- Use "will" for commitments; "may", "typically", "subject to" for non‑guaranteed outcomes
- Avoid absolute guarantees
- State when content is indicative, non‑binding, or not an implementation plan
